By accessing, browsing, registering an account, or otherwise using any feature of the SenEtizen System, you acknowledge that you have carefully read, completely understood, and agreed to be legally bound by these Terms and Conditions in their entirety. 2. Definition of Terms

To ensure clarity and avoid misinterpretation, the following terms used throughout this agreement shall be defined as follows: "System" or "Platform" refers to the SenEtizen web application, its databases, interfaces, and all associated digital services provided by the barangay; "User" refers to any individual who successfully creates an account, logs in, or otherwise accesses the System for any purpose; "Senior Citizen" refers to any verified resident of the barangay who is legally recognized as sixty (60) years of age or older and is seeking civic assistance through the System; "Authorized Representative" refers to a family member, guardian, or caregiver who has been granted explicit, verifiable permission to operate an account on behalf of a senior citizen; and "Barangay" refers to the local government unit, including its elected officials, appointed staff, and IT administrators managing the platform.

3. User Responsibilities and Account Registration

Users are strictly required to provide accurate, complete, current, and truthful information during the initial registration process and at all times when requesting barangay services, social welfare programs, or medical assistance. Because this system is tied to the allocation of public funds and government resources, users must ensure that their legal names, birth dates, residential addresses, and contact details are exactly as they appear on their official government-issued identification cards. Users are also responsible for proactively updating their digital profiles should there be any changes to their personal information, living arrangements, or residency status within the barangay.

The submission of false, forged, or misleading information, or any attempt to impersonate another individual—including registering on behalf of a senior citizen without their explicit knowledge and legally binding consent—is strictly prohibited and will be treated as a severe violation. Such actions will result in the immediate rejection of pending service requests, the indefinite suspension or permanent banning of the user’s account, and may subject the offending party to administrative sanctions, penalties, or criminal prosecution for falsification of public documents or identity theft under applicable Philippine laws.

4. Acceptable Use and Restrictions

The SenEtizen System is a dedicated local government resource funded by public money and shall only be used for legitimate, civic, and barangay-related transactions. This primarily includes, but is not limited to, requesting official barangay documents (such as certificates of residency or clearances), viewing critical community announcements, applying for local social welfare programs, scheduling medical assistance or check-ups, and other explicitly authorized services designed for the welfare of the elderly. Users must maintain a respectful and courteous demeanor when using any messaging or inquiry features to communicate with barangay staff.

Under no circumstances shall users utilize the platform for unlawful, fraudulent, commercial, or malicious purposes. Users are strictly prohibited from engaging in the following activities: (a) attempting to bypass security measures to gain unauthorized access to system resources, administrator dashboards, or the private data of other registered residents; (b) interfering with or intentionally disrupting system operations and servers through overwhelming requests or denial-of-service attacks; (c) uploading or transmitting destructive code, viruses, malware, or any other software intended to damage digital infrastructure; and (d) engaging in automated data scraping, mining, or any activity that compromises the integrity, availability, or overall security of the platform.

Users hold the primary and ultimate responsibility for safeguarding their account credentials, including their chosen usernames, passwords, and any secondary authentication codes. To maintain the highest level of security, users are strongly advised to create complex passwords and avoid logging into their SenEtizen accounts on public or shared computers. Sharing login credentials with unauthorized individuals, even close friends or unverified relatives, is strictly prohibited to prevent unauthorized access, data breaches, and the filing of fraudulent assistance requests.

The Barangay shall not be held legally or financially liable for any loss, damage, missed assistance allocations, or unauthorized transactions arising directly or indirectly from a user's negligence or failure to properly secure their account credentials. Any suspected account compromise, unauthorized access, or unusual activity—such as service requests you did not initiate—must be reported immediately to the Barangay Administration or the designated system IT administrator so that prompt preventive measures, such as temporary account freezing, can be implemented.

6. Data Privacy and Protection

The SenEtizen System processes and stores highly sensitive personal information, including health records, financial statuses, and exact residential locations. By registering for and using the platform, you provide your explicit consent to the collection, processing, storage, and sharing of your personal data by the Barangay solely for the specific purposes of delivering requested services, validating identity, and effectively implementing local senior citizen welfare programs.

All data processing shall be conducted in strict, unwavering compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173). The Barangay commits to implementing robust, industry-standard organizational, physical, and technical security measures to protect user data against unauthorized access, accidental alteration, unlawful disclosure, or destruction. PRIVACY POLICY

Effective Date: June 2026

The SenEtizen – Senior Citizen Assistance System highly values your privacy and is fully committed to protecting your personal data. This comprehensive Privacy Policy outlines how your personal and sensitive information is collected, processed, utilized, stored, and safeguarded by the Barangay Administration. All data processing activities conducted within this platform strictly adhere to the guidelines set forth by the National Privacy Commission (NPC) and are in full compliance with Republic Act No. 10173, otherwise known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012. By registering and utilizing the SenEtizen platform, you grant your explicit consent to the data practices described in this document.

1. Information We Collect

To effectively provide you with necessary barangay services, the system requires the collection of specific data points. The information we gather is divided into two categories:

  • Personal and Sensitive Information: This includes your full name, exact residential address, date of birth, age, contact numbers, email address, civil status, and official identification numbers (such as your OSCA ID or Senior Citizen ID number). In cases involving medical assistance requests, we may also collect necessary health-related information and medical records.
  • Documentary Requirements: Digital copies of documents you upload for verification purposes, such as valid government IDs, proof of billing, medical prescriptions, or certificates of residency.

2. Purpose and Use of Collected Information

The SenEtizen platform operates as a localized digital public service tool. Therefore, your personal information is collected and processed solely for legitimate barangay operations and public administration purposes. We use your data specifically to:

  • Accurately verify your identity and confirm your eligibility as a senior citizen resident of the barangay.
  • Process your requests for official documents, medical assistance, financial aid, and other social welfare programs efficiently.
  • Maintain an accurate and updated digital registry of senior citizens to aid the local government in the proper allocation of public funds and resources.
  • Communicate with you directly regarding the status of your requests, upcoming barangay events, health advisories, and emergency announcements.

Your information will never be used for commercial purposes, marketing, or any unauthorized activities outside the scope of barangay public service.

3. Data Security and Confidentiality

The Barangay takes the security of your personal data very seriously. We have implemented a combination of rigorous administrative, organizational, and technical safeguards to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, accidental loss, unlawful disclosure, alteration, or misuse.

Access to the backend database of the SenEtizen System is strictly restricted. Only officially designated and legally accountable barangay personnel, such as the IT Administrator, Barangay Secretary, and assigned Social Workers, are granted access to your data. These authorized individuals are bound by strict confidentiality agreements and are permitted to access your information only when absolutely necessary to perform their official duties.

4. Sharing and Disclosure of Information

Your personal information is kept strictly confidential within the barangay administration. We do not sell, rent, or trade your data to private third parties. However, your information may be shared or disclosed under the following specific circumstances:

  • When sharing data with higher government agencies (such as the DSWD, OSCA, or the Department of Health) is required to facilitate wider senior citizen assistance programs.
  • When mandated by law, court order, or a valid legal process executed by law enforcement agencies.
  • When necessary to protect the life, health, and immediate safety of the user during medical emergencies or disaster response situations.

5. Data Retention and Disposal

Personal information collected through the SenEtizen platform shall be retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the legal, administrative, and operational requirements of the barangay. Active accounts will have their data maintained to ensure seamless transactions. For inactive accounts, deceased residents, or individuals who have relocated outside the barangay, data will be archived in accordance with the retention limits set by the National Archives of the Philippines. Once the retention period expires, physical documents will be securely shredded, and digital records will be permanently and irretrievably deleted from our servers.

6. User Rights Under the Data Privacy Act

As a registered user and data subject, you are entitled to specific rights guaranteed by RA 10173. You have the right to be informed about how your data is being used. You may request access to the personal information we hold about you and demand the correction or updating of any inaccurate or outdated data in your profile. Furthermore, you have the right to object to the processing of your data, request the suspension, withdrawal, or secure destruction of your information from our system, and claim compensation if you suffer damages due to unauthorized data breaches.

7. Cookies, System Logs, and Technical Tracking

To ensure the system runs smoothly and securely, the SenEtizen platform automatically collects non-identifiable technical information when you access the site. This includes login timestamps, IP addresses, browser types, and activity logs. We also use "cookies" (small data files stored on your device) to keep you logged in during your session and to remember your interface preferences. These technical logs are used exclusively for security auditing, troubleshooting errors, preventing fraudulent access, and monitoring overall system performance. They are not used to track your activities on other websites.

8. Updates to this Privacy Policy

The Barangay reserves the right to periodically review and update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in national privacy laws, municipal ordinances, or significant upgrades to the SenEtizen system’s functionalities. Whenever substantial changes are made, we will notify users by posting a prominent announcement on the system's login page or dashboard. We strongly encourage you to review this policy regularly to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.

9. Contact Information and Data Protection Officer

If you have any questions, concerns, clarifications, or requests regarding your personal data or this Privacy Policy, please do not hesitate to reach out to the barangay administration. You may submit a formal inquiry to the designated Data Protection Officer (DPO) by visiting the Barangay Hall in person, utilizing the official contact numbers provided on the SenEtizen platform, or sending a message through the system's integrated helpdesk feature.
